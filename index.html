Hello,

I am Jacob Brown, a programmer, bowler, a learner, and a Scout. I am a Computer & Information Technology major at Purdue University with a minor in Management that has been working at MedSitter as a R&D Intern for almost a year. I have been writing code since early in middle school as I have always loved what computers can do, although, I would not say I was programming till Freshman year of Highschool, as before that I was just doing basic things messing around with different services like scratch, but in Freshman year I started taking classes in coding. The first class I took was Computer Science I, and at my highschool this class focused on teaching the basics of what was a coding language and how to use it for very simple applications, my school used Game Maker 2 for this. I went on to take every single computer class offered at my highschool at the time. I learned the basics of web design, cybersecurity, general coding priciples, and some basics of Java and JavaScript. I was lucky enough that I attended a highschool with a large internship program that I took part in durring my Senior year. Leading up to my last semester I was approched about an internship a place called MedSitter in their Software R&D department, of course as a highschooler with no work expirance in that field but wanting to join the field I took the offer right away. I still work there to this day getting close to 12 months. At Medsitter I have learned a lot and the team there is wonderful. Over the course of those 12 months any time I have found something I did not know, I have made a point to learn it as fast and as deep as possible, both inside and outside of work as it is all things I truly wish to learn and know. Outside of programming I have been bowling for 6 years, starting in 8th grade. I currently bowl for the Purdue Intercolleget Bowling Club on their travel roster. In highschool I bowled for the Noblesville High School Bowling Team, on both their JV and Varsity teams throughout the years. In highschool we traveled to state 3 times and won both the 2nd and 3rd appencices there. Sadly we did not make it out of Sectionals my Senior year but we all still had a blast and prepared the team for the next year. I am, at the end of everything, a scout. I am an Eagle Scout from Scouts BSA, I achieved this on December 20th, 2018 after completing all the ranks leading up to it and doing a project of helping the fostercare non-profit Hands Of Hope to re-organize their storage. I completed my project by designing, building, and installing custom wooden shelving units into their storage unit. I also am an Arrowman and a Firecrafter, an Arrowman is the general member rank for the Scouts BSA national honor society Order Of The Arrow, and Firecrafter is the highest standard position in the Indiana honor society Firecrafter. I am currently a Collegit Scouter of Troop 183, meaning I am an adult leader for the troop that attends college and thus normally can not be fully active. I have been in scouts since 2nd grade and I don't see myself truly ever leaving it. I love bowling, learning, programming, and helping others, I am Jacob Brown.
